# Intent And Searcher Job

Classify intent before choosing structure.

## Intent Classes

| Intent | Searcher job | Page obligation |
|---|---|---|
| Informational | Understand a concept or process | Answer clearly, early, and without forcing a product frame |
| How-to | Complete a task | Give steps, examples, tools, pitfalls, and outcome checks |
| Template | Get a usable artifact | Put the template/example near the top; avoid long preambles |
| Comparison | Choose between options | Define criteria, compare fairly, disclose tradeoffs |
| Alternatives | Replace a known option | Include real alternatives in the expected category |
| Commercial category | Shortlist tools/vendors | Provide criteria, category map, use cases, and honest fit |
| Troubleshooting | Fix a problem | Lead with likely causes and fixes, not background |
| Brand/query navigation | Reach or understand a known brand | Do not intercept with unrelated product copy |

## Expected Answer Set

For listicles, alternatives, and comparisons, ask what belongs in the answer set before deciding what the brand wants to promote.

- "ChatGPT alternatives" expects AI chatbots or tools used instead of ChatGPT.
- "Project management software" expects tools in that category, not generic productivity essays.
- "Template" queries expect downloadable/copyable artifacts, not a sales page with a buried template.

If the product does not belong in the expected answer set, it must not be ranked or framed as if it does.

## Intent Fit Checklist

- Primary intent is stated in one sentence.
- Secondary intents are listed but do not hijack the page.
- Page type matches the job.
- The outline reaches the answer before the pitch.
- The product role follows product fit, not ownership of the site.
