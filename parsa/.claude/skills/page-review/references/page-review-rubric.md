# Page Review Rubric

Score each area from 1 to 5. Use low scores to drive concrete rewrite instructions.

| Area | 5 means | 1 means |
|---|---|---|
| Usefulness | Reader can complete the job or make a decision | Page mainly delays, summarizes, or sells |
| Intent match | Page type and answer set match the query | Page routes the query into an unrelated funnel |
| Time to value | First real value appears early | Value is buried after intro/promo |
| E-E-A-T/proof | Claims are backed by experience, evidence, examples, or methodology | Claims are generic, unsupported, or stale |
| Voice | Specific, clear, tradeoff-aware, human | Generic, padded, fake-neutral, or AI-ish |
| Promotion fit | Product appears where it helps | Product distorts the answer |
| Fairness | Same standards for own product and others | Own product is favored by structure or vague criticism |
| Freshness | Tools, screenshots, dates, and claims are current | Stale names, outdated features, or broken schema |

## Recommendation Thresholds

- `Ship`: no score below 4, and promotion does not distort the answer.
- `Revise`: one or more 2-3 scores, but the page premise is sound.
- `Do not ship`: intent mismatch, deceptive product fit, unsupported comparison, or the page collapses without CTAs.

## Review Questions

- Does the page answer before selling?
- Is the expected answer set complete and honest?
- Are weaknesses specific for the brand and competitors?
- Are claims supported by proof or firsthand judgment?
- Is the reader's next step natural or forced?
