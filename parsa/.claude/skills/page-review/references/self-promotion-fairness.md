# Self-Promotion Fairness

Use this when reviewing alternatives pages, comparisons, listicles, category pages, and product-led informational content.

## Same-Rules Test

- Is the brand evaluated with the same criteria as competitors?
- Is the brand section similar in length and structure?
- Are brand limitations specific and useful?
- Are competitor limitations fair, sourced, or based on firsthand review?
- Would the order still make sense if names were hidden?
- Does the brand belong in the expected answer set?

## Structural Red Flags

- Brand ranked first without evidence.
- Brand section 2x or more longer than competitors.
- Competitor cons are specific, brand cons are vague.
- List includes irrelevant tools to justify the brand's inclusion.
- CTAs appear before criteria or useful options.
- Internal links route mostly to more self-promotional listicles.

## Acceptable Promotion

Promotion is acceptable when:

- The product is a direct or contextual fit.
- Criteria are clear before the pitch.
- The reader gets a useful recommendation even if they never click.
- The CTA is a next step, not a substitute for the answer.
