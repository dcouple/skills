# Good Vs Bad Editorial Systems

Use these examples to calibrate site-level findings.

## Good Scaled System

- Broad topics are allowed when pages satisfy the reader's job.
- Templates flex based on intent.
- Product placement varies by product fit.
- Listicles use real expected answer sets.
- Comparisons use the same criteria for every option.
- CTAs appear after value or at natural decision points.
- Weak or overlapping pages are merged, pruned, or redirected.
- Freshness is maintained for tools, screenshots, pricing, names, and schema.

## Bad Scaled System

- Every page routes to the product regardless of query.
- The same outline repeats across unrelated topics.
- The brand is always first, longest, and least criticized.
- Lists are padded with irrelevant entries.
- Competitor criticism is unsourced.
- CTAs appear before the promised answer.
- More templated pages are published during decline.
- Technical SEO uses unresolved variables or stale structured data at scale.

## Audit Phrase

Use this framing when appropriate:

`Topic breadth was not the core issue. The scalable pattern repeatedly betrayed intent by forcing product promotion into answers where it did not belong.`
