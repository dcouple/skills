# Good Vs Bad Examples

Use these examples to calibrate review comments.

## Useful-First Page

Good:
- First section answers the query.
- Criteria are visible before recommendations.
- Product copy supports, but does not replace, the answer.
- Limitations are specific for every option.
- Reader can act without clicking a CTA.

Bad:
- First sections sell the product before answering.
- Criteria are generic or absent.
- Own product receives much more space than alternatives.
- Competitor criticisms are unsourced.
- The page is mostly useless after removing CTAs.

## Alternatives Page

Good:
- Alternatives are real substitutes for the thing being replaced.
- The page explains why each alternative belongs.
- The brand appears only if it is truly an alternative.

Bad:
- The list includes adjacent tools that do not satisfy the query.
- The brand is placed first even though it is not a direct substitute.
- The list is padded to make the brand's inclusion feel normal.

## Review Language

Prefer:
- "Move this CTA after the first complete answer."
- "This tool does not belong in the expected answer set."
- "Apply the same limitation standard to your product."
- "Add firsthand proof or narrow the claim."

Avoid:
- "Make it more authoritative" without saying what evidence or specificity is missing.
