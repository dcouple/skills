# Site Audit Workflow

Use this workflow to avoid shallow explanations for traffic loss or content decay.

## Audit Sequence

1. Define scope: whole site, section, template family, competitor, or URL set.
2. Build inventory: sitemap, crawl, URL list, or export.
3. Segment inventory by section, template, topic, intent, page type, and product fit.
4. Merge performance data if available.
5. Separate demand loss from ranking loss.
6. Map important lost queries to current SERP winners.
7. Sample pages from winners and losers.
8. Identify recurring fingerprints: answer distortion, CTA overload, schema issues, stale names, list padding, repeated headings, or unfair comparison patterns.
9. Rank hypotheses by evidence strength.
10. Convert findings into action buckets and recovery sequence.

## Evidence Strength

Strong:
- URL-level ranking loss with current SERP replacement.
- Repeated issue across many pages in one section/template.
- Lost query still has demand and competitors still rank.
- Page samples show clear intent mismatch or template footprint.

Medium:
- Traffic drop aligns with a Google update.
- Section declined more than rest of domain.
- Similar pages share promotional or technical patterns.

Weak:
- Backlink changes without page/query evidence.
- General claims that AI overviews killed traffic.
- Topical overreach claims when core-topic pages also declined.

## Causality Rule

Treat Google update timing as correlation until supported by URL/query/SERP evidence.
