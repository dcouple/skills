# Template Footprint Risk

One mediocre page is a page problem. Thousands of structurally similar mediocre pages can become a section or site quality problem.

## Risk Signals

- Repeated intros that restate the keyword.
- Identical H2/H3 skeletons across many pages.
- Same CTA blocks in the same positions.
- Same product walkthrough embedded in unrelated topics.
- Own product section much longer than competitor sections.
- Vague limitations for own product and specific criticism for competitors.
- Dynamic year variables broken in schema or metadata.
- List padding to justify product placement.
- Generic pros/cons that could apply to any tool.
- Internal links routing to more promotional listicles instead of useful next steps.

## Section-Level Questions

- Is this template helping the reader or just scaling keyword capture?
- Would a human editor make the same structure for each query?
- Does the product role change based on intent, or is it always forced?
- Are weak pages being pruned, or is more of the same being published?

## Severity

Low:
- Pattern appears on a few pages but intent still matches.

Medium:
- Pattern repeats across a section and creates generic pages.

High:
- Pattern repeats across hundreds/thousands of URLs and distorts answer sets or product role.

Critical:
- The same weak pattern expanded during a decline instead of being pruned or rewritten.
